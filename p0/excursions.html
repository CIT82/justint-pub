Excursions on a 4-Day Carnival Mexican Cruise: Adventure and Relaxation in Equal Measure

One of the best things about a short Carnival cruise down the Mexican Riviera isn’t just the onboard fun, but the excursions waiting at each stop. A four-day itinerary typically sails from Long Beach, spends a day at Catalina Island, docks in Ensenada, and finishes with a sea day before returning to California. It’s a perfect “sampler” cruise: just enough time to dip into adventure, relaxation, and cultural flavor without needing a week off work. And the excursion menu Carnival offers makes the ports feel twice as rich.

On my first 4-day Mexican cruise, I treated each port day as a choose-your-own-adventure. Catalina was all about exploring the island vibe; Ensenada was a food and culture feast; the sea day was where I indulged in pure relaxation. Here’s what I learned, what I tried, and what’s still on my wish list for next time.

Catalina Island: A Playground Just Offshore

Catalina Island, just 22 miles off the California coast, feels like a world away the second you step ashore. The town of Avalon is dotted with pastel houses, golf carts whirring by like taxis, and the famous round Casino building looming over the bay. The excursions here fall into two main categories: adventure in the water or adventure on the island.

Snorkeling and Semi-Sub Adventures

For water lovers, Carnival sells guided snorkeling tours in Lover’s Cove, one of the island’s most protected marine areas. The water is cold but startlingly clear, and the kelp forests sway like curtains in the tide. Bright orange Garibaldi fish flash by in schools, and if you’re lucky, you might spot sea lions lounging on nearby rocks. I booked this on my first visit, and it felt surreal to swim so close to such lively sea life just a short ferry ride from L.A.

If snorkeling feels too intense, there’s the semi-submarine tour. You climb into a vessel with windows below the waterline and watch fish swim past without ever getting wet. Families with kids loved this — it was like an aquarium tour where the walls moved around you.

On-Land Options: Jeeps, Ziplines, and Views

Not a water person? Carnival’s catalog for Catalina also includes jeep eco-tours that take you into the rugged interior of the island. Here, bison (yes, bison!) roam wild, descendants of animals brought over for old Hollywood films. The jeep bumps over dusty roads, climbing to views that stretch across the Pacific. For thrill-seekers, the Catalina Zip Line Eco Tour is a must. Five separate lines carry you over canyons, each higher and faster than the last. By the time you reach the bottom, you’re buzzing with adrenaline.

For a slower pace, there’s always the glass-bottom boat tours, electric bike rentals, or just strolling Avalon’s waterfront shops. Carnival makes it easy to book in advance, but Catalina is also a port where you can comfortably wander on your own if you’d rather move at your own rhythm.

Ensenada: Baja Flavor and Culture

If Catalina feels like a playground, Ensenada feels like a feast. It’s Carnival’s classic Baja port, packed with food, history, and excursions that range from wine tours to adrenaline rides. I’d heard people call Ensenada a “budget” port, but I found it rich in flavor and atmosphere — the kind of place where one good taco can define your whole day.

La Bufadora Blowhole

One of the most famous excursions is the trip to La Bufadora, a natural sea geyser that shoots water up to 100 feet into the air as waves crash into a cave. The bus ride winds through desert hills before arriving at a cliffside market full of stalls selling blankets, silver jewelry, and street snacks. Watching the blowhole erupt is fun, but the street food — roasted corn, churros, and giant margaritas — was what made the trip memorable for me.

Wine Country Tours

Ensenada also sits near Mexico’s wine country, Valle de Guadalupe. Carnival offers wine tasting excursions that whisk you into the hills to sample local vintages at boutique wineries. Rolling vineyards under the sun felt a world away from the busy port. I tried rich reds paired with handmade cheeses, learning that Baja is now considered Mexico’s answer to Napa Valley.

Adventure on Land and Sea

For action seekers, Ensenada’s excursions deliver: ATV rides on dusty trails, horseback riding along the beach, or kayaking in the bay. I joined a small group horseback ride and found myself trotting past waves crashing on the sand. It was peaceful, exhilarating, and unlike anything I’d ever done before.

DIY Ensenada: Walking, Tacos, and Shopping

You don’t need an excursion to enjoy Ensenada. The downtown area is walkable, lined with shops and taco stands. I ducked into a street cart and ordered fish tacos — crispy, tender, served with cabbage and crema. They cost less than $2 each and were better than any gourmet seafood dish I’ve had stateside. Between the food, the music spilling from cantinas, and the warm welcome of locals, Ensenada delivered as much culture as any guided tour.

Sea Day: The Excursion Onboard

While not technically a “port excursion,” the sea day at the end of a 4-day cruise feels like one. It’s your chance to indulge in all the ship activities you skipped earlier. Carnival treats the ship itself as the destination, and the schedule bursts with trivia contests, spa specials, mixology classes, art auctions, and poolside games.

On my sea day, I started with a late breakfast, lingered over coffee, and then joined a tequila tasting seminar that walked us through shots, margaritas, and pairing tips. In the afternoon, I finally tried the water slides I’d been avoiding all week, then spent an hour in the Serenity adults-only retreat with a book. That night, I went all-in on the onboard entertainment: comedy show, late-night pizza, and a deck party under the stars. It felt like its own kind of port day, just without leaving the ship.

How to Choose Excursions: My Takeaways

With so many choices, it’s tempting to overbook. My advice: pick one big adventure per port, then leave time to wander. The beauty of Catalina and Ensenada is that both reward spontaneous exploration. Let the excursions give structure to your day, but don’t miss the joy of just soaking in the atmosphere with no schedule at all.

Carnival makes it simple to book excursions through the app or before you sail. Booking with the ship costs more than going direct, but it comes with the peace of mind that the ship will wait if your tour runs late. If you’re comfortable on your own, local vendors often offer similar experiences at lower prices — just make sure to keep an eye on “ship time,” which doesn’t always match your phone’s clock.

Lasting Impressions

A four-day Mexican cruise may be short, but the excursions make it feel expansive. Catalina gave me a taste of island adventure; Ensenada delivered bold flavors and culture; the sea day reminded me the ship itself is a destination. Together, they created a rhythm of action and rest, surprise and comfort.

Next time I’ll probably try something different — maybe the zipline in Catalina or wine country in Ensenada. But even if I repeated the same excursions, I know each would feel fresh, because the sea has a way of making every experience feel new. That’s the magic of cruising: it’s never just about the destination. It’s about the stories you bring home, stitched together from sun, salt air, and a little adventure at every port.
